Project 05 · UROP1000

Open-source digital IC design flow

Verilog HDL · RTL-to-GDS · Tiny Tapeout

An earlier undergraduate research project and the foundation for the RTL work above. I designed FSM-based logic (a clocked adder and a finite-state-machine digital lock) in Verilog, then took an RTL-to-GDS design through synthesis and place-and-route on the Tiny Tapeout platform — first hands-on exposure to the full open-source hardware flow, from HDL down to a physical layout.

HDL VerilogFlow RTL → synthesis → P&R → GDSPlatform Tiny Tapeout

The exposure to synthesis and place-and-route here is what made the later systolic-array and LUT/MAC projects legible to me at the hardware level — not just as RTL, but as something that maps onto real logic and timing.
